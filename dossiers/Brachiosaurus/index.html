BRACHIOSAURUS

Habitat

The Brachiosaurus lived in grasslands and marshes as their only source of food was vegatation. It was first actually theorized that they lived in water. Their large body was thought to be supported by water and not their small legs. This was proven to be false and that they were actually terrestrial.

Diet

Their herbivorous diet required them to eat hundreds of pounds of leaves a day. With such massive bodies and not being meat eaters they had adapted a longer neck to eat the leaves on higher trees without having to even move. The main staple to their diet was ginko leaves and coniferous trees.[1]

Adaptations and Behaviors

Their long necks provided them with the ability to eat leaves on higer trees and not even need to move from their spot to eat more leaves. The sheer size of these dinosaurs made them the least likely to be prey. The Brachiosaurus likely traveled in herds which further protected them.[5]

Size Range and Weight

The Brachiosaurus was one of the biggest dinosaurs of its time. A real giant among giants. Standing at 40 to 50 feet tall they could easily eat leaves from the tallest of trees. Their towering height came with a weight as little as 33 tons to as massive as 88 tons.[1]

When did it live?

They lived in the early to late Jurassic period. This period was around 160 to 140 million years ago.[2] Most of the sauropods were also around at this time such as the Brontosaurus and the Apatosaurus. For some unknown reason the sauropods dwindled and slowly died out during the start of the cretaceous period.

Distribution

Many of the brachiosaurus fossils were found in Central North America. They were mostly discovered in the Colorado River Valley. It is believed that Colorado was a vast plain during the Jurassic period. A sauropod such as the Brachiosaurus would thrive in that type of environment.[1]

Discovery

The Brachiosaurus was discovered in 1900 by Elmer Riggs[2] who also named it in 1903. He found the fossil in the Colorado River Valley where many more Brachiosaurus fossils were found. He named the sauropod after its towering neck and its long front limbs hence the name Arm Lizard.

Fossil Identification

Its fossil had many unique features such as its many vertebrae. Its skull had wide nose holes that were located to the top of the skull . This feature is why many believed it had lived in water. Their jaws were lined with chisel like teeth. Its front legs were longer than its back legs.[1]

Life Cycle

Brachiosauruses lived a long life. Up to one hundred years old![3] They would first start as hatchlings fresh out of their eggs. Then they would grow into juveniles, then sub-adults,  and then finally adults. It’s not exactly known when they become adults but it has been theorized that they would keep growing as sauropods could grow by six pounds a day.

Breeding Behavior

Not too much is known about these giant’s breeding behaviors. It has been theorized that they do nest together to protect themselves and their offspring.[4] They are giant which has led to a lot of speculation on how they actually bred.

Predators

There was no real threat to most Brachiosaurus. Unless they were not adults their massive size made them very hard to prey on. Along with this came their natural behaviors of living in a herd.[1] Their leather skin was also hard to tear which provided even more protection.

Communication

One of the biggest speculations to come from dinosaurs is their ways of communicating. The limited evidence of their past only led to speculations and ideas of what they would do. For the Brachiosaurus it was speculated that they made low-frequency sounds. It was also theorized that they would use their long necks as a visual signal.[5]
