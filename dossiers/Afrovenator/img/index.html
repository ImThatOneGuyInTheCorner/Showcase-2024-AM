Behaviors

The name afrovenator is a combination of the prefix “Afro” which means from Africa, and the base word venator which means “hunter”. The afrovenator has slender bones and sharp teeth. The dinosaur was also a bipedal creature meaning it can stand on its hind legs making it an effective predator.[2] It was assumed the afrovenator roared to show presence and dominance.

Predators and Danger

Stronger dinosaurs like Tyrannosaurus were more dominant and Natural disasters were the main predators. If the afrovenator did hunt alone it would make itself a target towards more dominant dinosaurs.

Fossils

Strong hindlegs but small forelimbs. They were expected to run decently fast because of how slim and slender their bones are. Teeth were sharp to cut into prey. Because of their teeth formation we know they didn't use their teeth for plants. Long slender neck with a strong jaw. [3]

Size

8-9 meters long (26-30 feet long). Weighing up to 1.5 tons.[2]

When and Where

It was discovered by Paul Sereno. Found in Niger, Africa. He took an expedition in 1993 to Niger, Africa. They went from Chicago to London to get materials for the expedition then traveled across the Mediterranean by sea. Each bone must be carefully extracted however they didn't have lifting equipment so it had to be pulled out of the ground by hand. The remains were discovered in 1993 in the Tiourarén Formation of the department of Agadez in Niger.[4] Findings were only ever found in Africa.
