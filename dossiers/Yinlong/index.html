Identification

Yinlong is a small dinosaur [2] reaching 3.9 ft long and having a 22lbs in body mass. Yinlong has a small rostral bone on the end of the upper jaw, identifying Yinlong as a part of the ceratopsian. Yinlong had long robust hind limbs and shorter slender forelimbs with three-fingered hands. The skull has several features, like the squamous bone of the skull roof, which was previously thought to be unique to pachycephalosaurians.

Habitat, Feeding & Diet, & Size

Yinlong lived in a terrestrial habitat[1] which had forests, grassland, desert, shorelines, and wetlands. Yinlong’s main diet [3] and feeding was aligned with herbivores which consisted of plants and foliage. Yinglong’s size was on the smaller size for dinosaurs reaching the length of 3.9 ft, and having a body mass of 22 pounds.

Discovery, Distribution, Description, & Fossil Record

Xu Xing, Catherine Forster, Jim Clark, and Mo Jinyou were a mixed team of American and Chinese paleontologists[2] that discovered and named Yinlong in 2006. So far paleontologists only found one specimen of Yinlong in Xinjiang Province, China[3]. Yinlong’s fossils record way back to 159 million to 154 million years ago during the Oxfordian and Kimmeridgian stages of the Late Jurassic Epoch. Yinlong’s skull signified that they were a part of the ceratopsian family. Their teeth and limb structure gave evidence they were only herbivores.

Time Period Yinlong lived in

Yinlong lived in the Late Jurassic Epoch[2] , when it was warmer and there were no ice caps. Forest and vegetation grew close to the poles. The Late Jurassic Epoch was also the time when Pangaea broke up into two supercontinents, Laurasia in the North and Gondwana in the south. Some examples[4] of dinosaurs that lived in the same period would be Agilisaurus, Allosaurus, Apatosaurus, and many more.
