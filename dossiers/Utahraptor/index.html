THE UTAHRAPTOR

  • Genus: Utahraptor
  • Species: Utahraptor ostrommaysorum, meaning "Utah's predator" (a single species)
  • Family: Dromaeosauridae (theropod dinosaurs commonly known as "raptors")
  • Suborder: Theropoda
  • Order: Saurischia
  • Subclass: Avetheropoda
  • Class: Sauropsida
  • Subphylum: Vertebrata
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Kingdom: Animalia

classification & distribution

The Utahraptor, a dinosaur species, roamed the Earth during the Early Cretaceous period, approximately 135 to 130 million years ago. It inhabited what is now the United States, specifically the region of Utah. Evidence of its existence has been found in the Cedar Mountain Formation, Utah, a site known for its diverse ecosystem and paleo-findings, including other dinosaur species like ankylosaurs and iguanodonts. It is important to note that the distribution of Utahraptor during its time was limited to the areas where its fossils have been discovered. Therefore, given the incomplete nature of the fossil record, it is plausible that Utahraptors lived in other regions that have yet to be discovered or studied.

behaviors & adaptations

The extinction of the Utahraptor is believed to be caused by the changing environment of the Earth, particularly the cooling down of the planet, which the species was unable to adapt to. This is thought to be the primary reason for the disappearance of Utahraptors worldwide after a span of around 5 million years. The Utahraptor was a large, feathered, actively predatory theropod with warm-blooded characteristics. It is speculated that packs of Utahraptors may have lived in forested or desert/prairie areas where hunting was abundant. It's worth noting that the question of whether the Utahraptor was warm-blooded or cold-blooded is still up for debate, as there is insufficient information to confirm either.

special features & identification

The Utahraptor is distinguishable from the famous velociraptor due to its significantly larger size. It had a sleek, feathered, bipedal body, with a heavily built, ground-dwelling structure. The Utahraptor had a narrow, sharply defined snout and forward-looking eyes. Its jaws contained small, serrated teeth that were used to dispatch its prey. The massive raptor also possessed a large "killing claw" on its second toe, which it used to capture and kill its prey.

fossils, discoveries, & size

who discovered the infamous killer?

The Utahraptor was discovered by Jim Jensen in 1975. However, his initial findings were too fragmented to accurately identify the dinosaur. It wasn't until 1991 that the Utahraptor was fully uncovered during a dig sponsored by the Prehistoric Museum, led by James Kirkland, Robert Gaston, and Donald Burge. All the discoveries were made in Utah, hence the name Utah-raptor. To this day, we continue to make great findings in this dinosaur's ancient history. Games like Jurassic World: The Game and Path of Titans feature this great dinosaur for players to discover and compare its size, providing a fun and interactive way to learn about the Utahraptor.

predators & prey

Whether or not the Utahraptor hunted in packs is a topic of debate among researchers, as its large size and fierce nature make it capable of hunting alone. However, many sources and research scientists believe that the Utahraptor did hunt in packs. It is also believed that the species followed a typical Dino-family system, as depicted in movies, such as defending eggs or bringing back food to the nest. As the dominant carnivore in its ecosystem, the Utahraptor ate practically any dinosaur it desired, including Iguanodonts, a large herbivore dinosaur species.

what fossil history are we talking about?

James Kirkland, the paleontologist for the state of Utah, named and uncovered the Utahraptor in 1993. In 2018, the Utahraptor was designated as the official state dinosaur. Kirkland hypothesized that the Utahraptors at the Stikes Quarry site got stuck in sinking pits of quicksand while attacking plant-eating dinosaurs that were already trapped there. Their death and submersion in sand is the reason why their bodies were so well-preserved throughout the passing millions of years.

latest fossil discoveries

In a recent discovery, James Kirkland and a small team successfully removed a massive 18,000-pound block of Utahraptor fossils from Stikes Quarry. His latest study includes an updated estimate of the dinosaur's age, which is now believed to be 135 million years old, approximately 10 million years older than previously believed.

life cycle, breeding, & habitat

about breeding and life cycle

It is believed that Utahraptors lived a life cycle similar to other dinosaurs. They likely started off in an egg and went through an incubation process. The lifespan of a Utahraptor is unclear, with some sources suggesting a range of 70-80 years, while others estimate a little over 20 years. As expected, there are no known special breeding behaviors. However, a recent discovery in the mega block mentioned earlier includes the remains of six Utahraptors, including an adult measuring 16 feet long, four youngsters, and a possible baby Utahraptor measuring only 1 foot long. The mega block's unique preservation of the bodies over millions of years could be a coincidence, but Kirkland believes otherwise and posits that it was a Utahraptor family. This discovery is significant as it could be the start of understanding the behavior of Utahraptors in an unexpected way.

size range & ways our dinosaur communicated

The Utahraptor was a large dinosaur, weighing over half a ton to a little over a ton, standing 6 feet tall and measuring up to 23 feet in length. It was significantly larger than the famous velociraptor and is believed to have been one of the smartest dinosaurs of all time. The Dromaeosaurs, in particular, were one of the most intelligent groups of dinosaurs ever to exist. This is known because paleontologists use the animal's Encephalization Quotient, or EQ, to determine relative dinosaur intelligence. For reference, an EQ of troodontids is about 6, where dromaesaurs are not far behind with an EQ of 5.8. For a more relative comparison, humans have the highest EQ of 7.4-7.8.

other utahraptor species & relative habitatual behaviors

What's interesting about the Utahraptor is that we have only discovered one species of this dinosaur, known as Utahraptor ostrommaysorum, which means "Utah's predator." The Utahraptor inhabited the plains of North America, specifically floodplains and open woodlands with riverine forests. It was not believed to be very fast, so it likely used cover to lie in wait for prey. Utahraptor packs also likely lived in forests or deserts/prairies where hunting wouldn't be problematic. It is estimated that they had a certain amount of territory, around 3 miles all around, which would have been near a water source, suggesting that Utahraptors may have also eaten fish.
