Giganotosaurus

Classification

Habitat

Lived in a tropical and warm climate with lots of vegetation containing lots of herbivore dinosaurs for the giganotosaurus to prey on. The giganotosaurus lived near the coastal plains and inland areas, using the environment for both food and water.

Size Range

The giganotosaurus measured to be about 41 feet meters long with a staggering height of 23 feet high and also weighing about 8.8 tons at max. This dinosaur had 76 teeth with each tooth being 8 inches long.

Distribution

Most of the dinosaur fossils were found in Argentina, however this isn’t official, since fossils have only been found in Argentina and can possibly be found in other places.

Time Era

The giganototsaurus lived aduring the early Cretaceous (Mesozoic Era) about 145-66 million years ago. This meant that this apex predator never came into contact with a T-Rex.

Discovery

Discovery of Giganotosaurus

The giganotosaurus was discovered by Rubén Dario Carolini in the Neuquén Province of Patagonia (southern Argentina) after he stumbled upon the lower leg bone of the dino. After being classified as a new genus and species, it was named Giganotosaurus carolinii.

Fossil Description

Somewhat similar to the T-Rex and also related to other theropods, the giganotosaurus walked on two strong hind limbs, also being able to run at 31 mph. It also had an abnormally large head containing razor sharp teeth as well as 3 razor claws.

Identification

The giganotosaurus can be identified by its large and elongated skull compared to the T-Rex. The skull of a giganotosaurus was unique as it had a low skull with a deep 92 cm long upper jaw. It also had a chin displayed as its lower jaw.

Fossil Record

Most of the giganotosaurus fossils were found in the Patagonian region of Argentina. There is limited data on other whereabouts of the giganotosaurus fossils.

Diet and Behavior

Feeding and Diet

The giganotosaurus preyed on larger herbivores such as sauropods because there was an abundance of herbivores during the Late Cretaceous era, due to the vegetation abundance.

Behavior and adaptations

When hunting the, the giganotosaurus was an apex predator that hunted in packs or as solitary predators. Unlike the T-Rex, the giganotosaurus adapted to have a good sense of smell and vision. It also used its environment to ambush prey, since their habitat could keep them hidden.

Breeding Behaviors

There’s limited details about the breeding behaviors of the giganotosaurus, however it probably laid eggs like other theropod dinosaurs.

Communication and Life Cycle

Communication

Like other theropods, depending on the circumstances such as the prey availability, it was believed that Giganotosaurus were in packs or were alone.

Predators

The giganotosaurus had no natural predators because of its size. However, when it’s a juvenile, it was vulnerable to larger dinos such as a Carnotaurus. When the giga grew to an adult it became an apex predator that didn’t live near or ever come into contact with another dangerous apex predator.
