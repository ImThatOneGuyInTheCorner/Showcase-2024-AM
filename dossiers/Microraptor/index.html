Microraptor

Hello! This website has information about the Microraptor.

Classification

  • Genus: Microraptor
  • Species: zhaoianus, hanqingi, gui
  • Family: Dromaeosauridae
  • Suborder: Theropoda
  • Class: Unknown
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Kingdom: Animalia

Size Range

The range of height was 2.5 to 3.1 feet, and the weight was less than 3lbs, equal to 1kg.

Habitat and Distribution

The microraptor was only found in China. It may have resided in forest areas.

Identification

The microraptor was the smallest and most birdlike of all dinosaur species. Due to its size, it received its name, which means “small thief” in Greek. According to researchers, most of them were black and glossy, which may have seemed like an iridescence found in peacocks and hummingbirds. As for its small teeth, it was both serrated (sawlike) and non-serrated. It was equipped with long flight feathers on all its 4 limbs and moved with two legs. Although, it is unknown if it can fly or glide.

Discovery

The person/group who discovered the microraptor was unknown. The date of discovery is 2000.

Time Period and Contemporaries

It lived in the Early Cretaceous period, which happened 125 to 120 million years ago.

Feeding and Diet

The microraptor would have a carnivorous diet due to its teeth. Here is a list of their prey:

  • Mainly, small birds who shared trees
  • Reptiles, such as small extinct lizards
  • Insects
  • Fish
  • Other mammals

Behaviors and Adaptations

The microraptor was nocturnal, which meant it was only active at night. It may have glided like a flying squirrel, but it is unsure if it had the capability to fly.

Communication

Microraptors were in solitude. They were also in groups. It is unsure how they communicated with each other.

Life History Cycle

Its life history cycle is not well known. By an adult, it reaches to 2.5-3-1 ft, and weighs less than 3lbs, equal to 1 kg.

Predators

The microraptor may have been hunted by the jidapterus, a genus of azhdarchoid pterodactyloid pterosaur. It also would have competed for prey with many similar-sized prehistoric birds.

Fossils

All of the fossils were found in the Jiufotang Formation in Liaoning, China. This place was an area of forests where many prehistoric birds and other bird-like dinosaurs that are similar to the microraptor lived.
