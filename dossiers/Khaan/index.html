The Khaan

Khaan mckennai, a dinosaur discovered in 2001 by James M. Clark in the Woodlands of the Nemegt Basin in Mongolia, which is located in central Asia. Khaan had a maximum size of approximately 110 centimeters long (around 6 feet). This small theropod dinosaur resembles a small bird-like creature that has feathers. It likely had a carnivorous diet, primarily consisting of small vertebrates such as mammals, lizards, and possibly other small dinosaurs. Khaan was an egg-layer and showed nesting behaviors. The exact vocalization abilities of Khaan are not known, some theropods may have used their mouths to make noise as a means of communication with each other.

Predator

Although there is no direct evidence for predators the Khaan would have faced, based on educated guesses of known predators who had lived in the area. Khaan was a small dinosaur, it would have been vulnerable to predators of larger theropods such as the Tarbosaurus.
